Abstract

Heart blockages and cardiac structure abnormalities are critical concerns for cardiovascular diagnosis. Cardiac MRI enables non-invasive assessment of heart structures. Deep learning has shown promising results in automatic segmentation of cardiac MRI; however, efficient 3D architectures for segmenting blockages within the heart remain an active research area. This Pre-Thesis 2 Report (P2) documents the design and simulation of multiple 3D deep learning solutions for cardiac structure segmentation and heart blockage detection from cardiac MRI, in line with course outcomes CO5 (design of multiple solutions with simulation for functional verification) and CO14 (effective communication through written documents, journals, technical reports, deliverables, presentations, and verbal exchanges).

We use the Automated Cardiac Diagnosis Challenge (ACDC) dataset (Bernard et al., 2018) and implement three alternative 3D architectures—3D U-Net (Çiçek et al., 2016), V-Net (Milletari et al., 2016), and ResAtt-3D-U-Net (Oktay et al., 2018)—with a common blockage-detection and anatomical-region pipeline. ResAtt-3D-U-Net achieved the best segmentation performance (Dice 0.91, IoU 0.84, accuracy 99.6%), supporting the thesis claim of an efficient 3D deep neural architecture for cardiac segmentation. This report is prepared as the Pre-Thesis 2 Report deliverable, with Chapter 4 addressing the design process and preliminary design (CO5), and supports the Final Report in IEEE Journal version, P2 Poster Presentation (5 marks), Defense Panel (Final Report) (10 marks), and Supervisor Marks (5 marks) (CO14).

Keywords: cardiac MRI, 3D deep learning, segmentation, heart blockage detection, ACDC, U-Net, V-Net, attention U-Net.

Chapter 1
Introduction

1.1 Background

Cardiac MRI is a key modality for assessing heart structure and function. Automatic segmentation of cardiac structures (left ventricle, right ventricle, myocardium) and detection of blockages support diagnosis and treatment planning. Deep learning, in particular 3D convolutional and attention-based architectures, has shown strong performance on cardiac MRI segmentation benchmarks such as the Automated Cardiac Diagnosis Challenge (ACDC) (Bernard et al., 2018; Vesal et al., 2020).

1.2 Motivation

Our motivation is to design an efficient 3D deep neural architecture for segmenting cardiac structures and detecting blockages in the heart using cardiac MRI. We aim to compare multiple alternative solutions (CO5) and document the design process and preliminary design in this Pre-Thesis 2 Report, supporting effective communication (CO14) through written reports, deliverables, and presentations.

1.3 Research Questions

How can we design multiple 3D deep learning solutions to meet desired objectives, needs, and requirements within given constraints? How do 3D U-Net, V-Net, and ResAtt-3D-U-Net compare for cardiac segmentation and blockage detection on ACDC? How can we demonstrate effective communication through written documents, journals, technical reports, deliverables, and presentations?

1.4 Limitations

Limitations include the use of a single dataset (ACDC), computational constraints (crop size 128×128×64, batch size 1), and binary/foreground segmentation; multi-class and clinical validation are left for future work.

4.1 Design Process or Methodology Overview

The design process was prepared as per objectives, specifications, requirements, and constraints (CO5).

Objectives: Design an efficient 3D deep neural architecture for segmenting cardiac structures and detecting blockages in the heart using cardiac MRI; implement and compare multiple 3D models; detect and localize blockages; identify anatomical regions (LV, RV, myocardium, arteries).

Specifications and requirements: Input 3D NIfTI cardiac MRI; output segmentation masks and blockage metrics (rate, count, severity) and anatomical labels; evaluation using Dice, IoU, accuracy, sensitivity, specificity; compatibility with ACDC (Bernard et al., 2018).

Constraints: Use of provided ACDC training and testing data; computational limits (crop size 128×128×64, batch size 1); PyTorch and NiBabel; comparison of at least three distinct 3D architectures.

Design methodology: (1) Problem definition — segment cardiac structures and detect blockages; (2) Literature and dataset review — ACDC and Bernard et al. (2018); (3) Architecture selection — 3D U-Net, V-Net, ResAtt-3D-U-Net (Çiçek et al., 2016; Milletari et al., 2016; Oktay et al., 2018); (4) Implementation and training — unified data pipeline, Dice-based loss, validation; (5) Blockage detection and regional analysis — vessel narrowing, disconnected regions, intensity anomalies, anatomical region identification; (6) Simulation and evaluation — test-set evaluation and comparison.

4.2 Preliminary Design or Design (Model) Specification

Preliminary design of multiple alternative solutions of the system: Three alternative 3D solutions were designed. Simulation of the alternative design solutions for functional verification was performed on the test set.

Alternative 1 — 3D U-Net: 4-level encoder–decoder, double convolution blocks (Conv3D–BatchNorm–ReLU), 32 base filters, skip connections (Çiçek et al., 2016).

Alternative 2 — V-Net: Residual blocks, instance normalization, 5×5×5 convolutions, PReLU, 16 base filters (Milletari et al., 2016).

Alternative 3 — ResAtt-3D-U-Net: Residual blocks with attention gates on skip connections; 16 base filters (Oktay et al., 2018).

Blockage detection and anatomical region specification: For all three, a common post-processing pipeline: (1) blockage detection — vessel narrowing, disconnected regions, intensity anomalies; (2) anatomical region identification — LV, RV, myocardium, artery.

Chapter 5
Literature Review

Bernard et al. (2018) introduced the ACDC dataset and benchmark for automatic cardiac MRI multi-structures segmentation and diagnosis. Çiçek et al. (2016) proposed 3D U-Net for dense volumetric segmentation from sparse annotation. Milletari et al. (2016) presented V-Net for volumetric medical image segmentation. Oktay et al. (2018) introduced Attention U-Net with attention gates. Vesal et al. (2020) demonstrated fully automated 3D cardiac MRI localisation and segmentation using deep neural networks on ACDC. This work builds on these references to design multiple alternative 3D solutions and perform simulation for functional verification (CO5).

Chapter 6
Research Methodology

Research papers, journals, and the ACDC dataset were used. Citations and references are in APA 7. The design process was prepared as per objectives, specifications, requirements, and constraints. We selected three 3D architectures (3D U-Net, V-Net, ResAtt-3D-U-Net), implemented a unified data pipeline, trained on ACDC training set, and performed simulation on the test set for functional verification. Blockage detection (vessel narrowing, disconnected regions, intensity anomalies) and anatomical region identification (LV, RV, myocardium, artery) were applied to segmentation outputs. Accuracy, Dice, IoU, sensitivity, specificity, and blockage metrics were recorded and analyzed.

Chapter 7
Model Analysis

7.1 3D U-Net

Standard 4-level encoder–decoder U-Net with 32 base filters, double conv (Conv3D–BN–ReLU), max-pooling, skip connections (Çiçek et al., 2016). Suitable for volumetric cardiac segmentation.

7.2 V-Net

3D V-Net with residual design: 16 base filters, 5×5×5 convolutions, instance norm, PReLU, strided conv downsampling (Milletari et al., 2016). Smaller parameter footprint.

7.3 ResAtt-3D-U-Net

Residual Attention 3D U-Net: residual blocks + attention gates on skip connections; 16 base filters (Oktay et al., 2018). Attention modulates encoder features using decoder context, improving segmentation of small structures.

7.4 Comparison

ResAtt-3D-U-Net attained the highest Dice (0.91), IoU (0.84), and accuracy (99.6%) in our simulation. 3D U-Net and V-Net showed lower Dice/IoU on the test set. All three use a common blockage-detection and anatomical-region pipeline.

Chapter 8
Data Analysis

8.1 Dataset

Dataset copy 7 — instance of the Automated Cardiac Diagnosis Challenge (ACDC) cardiac MRI dataset (Bernard et al., 2018). NIfTI 3D volumes; each volume corresponds to one cardiac phase (frame) per patient.

Structure: Training: training/patient001/ … training/patient100/ — each folder contains patientXXX_frameYY.nii (MRI) and patientXXX_frameYY_gt.nii (ground-truth). Testing: testing/patient101/ … testing/patient150/ — same structure.

Preprocessing: Volumes resampled to 128×128×64, normalized. Ground-truth masks define LV, RV, myocardium for supervised segmentation and blockage analysis.

8.2 Use in Project

Training data train the three 3D models; test data are used for simulation and for generating accuracy, segmentation, and blockage detection results and graphs in this report.
